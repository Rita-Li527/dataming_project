RECORD LAYOUT

Below are fields in the order that they appear on the records:
datetimeThe given day that the weather data is collected
tempmaxMaximum temperature on a given day
tempminMinimum temperature on a given day
tempAverage temperature on a given day in celsius
feelslikemaxSensory maximum temperature on a given day
feelslikeminSensory minimum temperature on a given day
feelslikeSensory maximum temperature on a given day
dewThe temperature to which the air must cool for it to become completely saturated with water
humidityThe amount of water vapor in the air, measured in percentage
precipThe amount of precipitation that fell or is predicted to fall in the specified time period. The values are indicated in inches.
precipprobThe possibility of precipitation for the given time period expressed as a percentage change from 0-100%. This applies to the forecast queries only
precipcoverPrecipitation coverage is the proportion of time for which measurable precipitation was recorded during the time period, expressed as a percentage
preciptypeThe type(s) of precipitation expected. Possible values include rain, snow, freezing rain and ice
snowThe amount of new snow that has fallen in the time period
snowdepthThe average amount of snow currently on the ground for the time period
windgustWind gust is the maximum wind speed measures over a short amount of time (typically less than 20 seconds
windspeedWind speed is typically measured 10m above ground in a location with no nearby obstructions
winddirThe wind direction indicates the direction from where the wind is blowing from. The units of the wind direction is degrees from north.
sealevelpressureThe atmospheric pressure at a location that removes reduction in pressure due to the altitude of the location. This is expressed in millibars.
cloudcoverThe amount of sky that is covered by cloud expressed as a percentage for all altitudes
visibilityThe distance that can be seen in daylight expressed in miles or kilometers
solarradiationThe amount of solar radiation per unit area per second, typically measured in Watts per meter squared
solarenergyThe total energy from the sun that builds up over an hour or day
uvindexA value between 0 and 10 indicating the level of ultraviolet (UV) exposure
severeriskA value between 0 and 100 representing the risk of convective storms (e.g. thunderstorms, hail and tornadoes)
sunriseThe exact time of sunrise
sunsetThe exact time of sunset
moonphaseA decimal value representing the current moon phase between 0 and 1 where 0 represents the new moon, 0.5 represents the full moon. The full cycle can be represented as: 0 – new moon; 0-0.25 - waxing crescent; 0.25 – first quarter; 0.25-0.5 – waxing gibbous; 0.5 - full moon; 0.5-0.75 - waning gibbous; 0.75 - last quarter; 0.75-1 - waning crescent.
conditionsShort text about the weather
descriptionDescription of the weather for the day
iconA weather icon
stationsList of weather stations sources
